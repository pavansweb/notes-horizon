# Coordination Compounds

## Topic Breakdown
### 1. Basic Terminology
#### 1.1 Coordination Entity and Central Atom
- The central metal ion is typically a transition metal.
#### 1.2 Ligands and Denticity
- Types: Monodentate, Bidentate (e.g., $en$, $ox^{2-}$), Polydentate (e.g., $EDTA^{4-}$).
- Ambidentate Ligands: Can bond through two different atoms (e.g., $SCN^-/NCS^-, NO_2^-/ONO^-$).
#### 1.3 Coordination Number and Oxidation State
- CN: Total number of ligand donor atoms directly bonded to the metal.

### 2. Nomenclature and Isomerism
#### 2.1 IUPAC Rules
- Order: Cation first, then anion. Ligands alphabetically, then metal with oxidation state in Roman numerals.
#### 2.2 Structural Isomerism
- Ionization, Solvate (Hydrate), Linkage, and Coordination Isomerism.
#### 2.3 Stereoisomerism
- Geometrical: $cis/trans$ and $fac/mer$ (for $MA_3B_3$ octahedral).
- Optical: Enantiomers in octahedral complexes with bidentate ligands.

### 3. Bonding Theories
#### 3.1 Valence Bond Theory (VBT)
- Hybridization ($sp^3, dsp^2, d^2sp^3, sp^3d^2$) and geometry.
- Inner orbital ($d^2sp^3$) vs. Outer orbital ($sp^3d^2$) complexes.
#### 3.2 Crystal Field Theory (CFT)
- Splitting of d-orbitals in Octahedral and Tetrahedral fields.
- Crystal Field Stabilization Energy (CFSE).
- Spectrochemical Series: $I^- < Br^- < SCN^- < Cl^- < S^{2-} < F^- < OH^- < C_2O_4^{2-} < H_2O < NCS^- < edta^{4-} < NH_3 < en < CN^- < CO$.

## Formulas & Laws
- **Effective Atomic Number (EAN):** $EAN = Z - \text{Oxidation State} + 2 \times \text{Coordination Number}$
- **Spin-only Magnetic Moment:** $\mu = \sqrt{n(n+2)} \text{ BM}$ (where $n$ is number of unpaired electrons)
- **CFSE (Octahedral):** $CFSE = [-0.4 n_{t_{2g}} + 0.6 n_{e_g}] \Delta_o + nP$
- **Relation between $\Delta_t$ and $\Delta_o$:** $\Delta_t \approx \frac{4}{9} \Delta_o$

## JEE Focus & Common Traps
- **Color in Complexes:** Arises from d-d transitions. Remember that $d^0$ and $d^{10}$ complexes are usually colorless.
- **Strong vs. Weak Field Ligands:** Strong field ligands (like $CN^-, CO$) cause pairing of electrons, leading to low-spin complexes.
- **Chelate Effect:** Complexes with chelating ligands (bidentate/polydentate) are significantly more stable than those with monodentate ligands.
- **Synergic Bonding:** In metal carbonyls, back-donation from metal d-orbitals to the $\pi^*$ orbitals of $CO$ strengthens the $M-C$ bond and weakens the $C-O$ bond.
\n\n## Typed Problems (JEE Main PYQ Based)
### Type 1: Crystal Field Splitting Energy (CFSE)
- **Q1.** Calculate CFSE for a $d^6$ high spin octahedral complex.
- **Concept:** CFSE = $[-0.4n_{t_{2g}} + 0.6n_{e_g}]\Delta_o + nP$.
- **Solution:**
  1. High spin $d^6 \implies t_{2g}^4 e_g^2$.
  2. CFSE = $[-0.4(4) + 0.6(2)]\Delta_o = [-1.6 + 1.2]\Delta_o = -0.4\Delta_o$.

### Type 2: Isomerism in Coordination Compounds
- **Q1.** How many geometrical isomers are possible for $[Pt(NH_3)(Py)(Cl)(Br)]$?
- **Concept:** For a square planar complex of type $[Mabcd]$, 3 geometrical isomers exist.
- **Solution:**
  1. The isomers are formed by keeping one ligand fixed and varying the one trans to it.
  2. Three isomers: (NH3 trans to Cl), (NH3 trans to Br), (NH3 trans to Py).
