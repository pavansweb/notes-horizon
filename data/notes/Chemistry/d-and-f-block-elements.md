# d-Block and f-Block Elements

## Topic Breakdown
### 1. d-Block Elements (Transition Metals)
#### 1.1 General Characteristics
- Variable oxidation states due to $(n-1)d$ and $ns$ energy proximity.
- Formation of colored ions via d-d transitions.
- Paramagnetism and complex formation.

#### 1.2 Important Compounds
- **Potassium Dichromate ($K_2Cr_2O_7$):** Preparation from chromite ore and oxidizing properties.
- **Potassium Permanganate ($KMnO_4$):** Preparation from pyrolusite ore; oxidizing action in acidic, neutral, and alkaline media.

### 2. f-Block Elements (Inner Transition Metals)
#### 2.1 Lanthanoids (4f series)
- **Lanthanoid Contraction:** Cumulative decrease in atomic/ionic radii. Causes include poor shielding by 4f electrons.
- Chemical reactivity and common $+3$ oxidation state.

#### 2.2 Actinoids (5f series)
- Radioactive elements with more variable oxidation states than lanthanoids.
- Actinoid contraction is greater than lanthanoid contraction.

## Formulas & Laws
- **Magnetic Moment (Spin-only):** $\mu = \sqrt{n(n+2)} \text{ BM}$, where $n$ is the number of unpaired electrons.

## JEE Focus & Common Traps
- **Color and Magnetism:** Always relate these to unpaired d-electrons.
- **KMnO4 and K2Cr2O7:** Master the balanced equations for their oxidizing actions.
\n\n## Typed Problems (JEE Main PYQ Based)
### Type 1: Magnetic Moment Calculation
- **Q1.** Calculate the spin-only magnetic moment of $Fe^{2+}$ ion ($Z=26$).
- **Concept:** $\mu = \sqrt{n(n+2)} BM$, where $n$ is the number of unpaired electrons.
- **Solution:**
  1. $Fe: [Ar] 3d^6 4s^2 \implies Fe^{2+}: [Ar] 3d^6$.
  2. $3d^6$ configuration: $\uparrow\downarrow, \uparrow, \uparrow, \uparrow, \uparrow \implies n=4$.
  3. $\mu = \sqrt{4(4+2)} = \sqrt{24} \approx 4.9 BM$.

### Type 2: Potassium Permanganate Reactions
- **Q1.** What is the equivalent mass of $KMnO_4$ in acidic medium? (Molecular mass = $M$).
- **Concept:** $Eq. mass = \frac{M}{n-factor}$. In acidic medium, $MnO_4^- + 8H^+ + 5e^- \rightarrow Mn^{2+} + 4H_2O$.
- **Solution:**
  1. Change in oxidation state of $Mn$ is from $+7$ to $+2$.
  2. $n-factor = 5$.
  3. $Eq. mass = M/5$.
