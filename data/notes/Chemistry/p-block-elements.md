# p-Block Elements (Groups 13-18)

## Topic Breakdown
### 1. General Trends
#### 1.1 Inert Pair Effect
- Stability of lower oxidation states increases down the group for heavier elements (e.g., $Tl^+$ is more stable than $Tl^{3+}$).

#### 1.2 Anomalous Properties
- Second-period elements (B, C, N, O, F) differ from their groups due to small size, high electronegativity, and absence of d-orbitals.

### 2. Group Specific Highlights
#### 2.1 Group 13 & 14
- Boron: Borax, Orthoboric acid, and **Diborane** ($B_2H_6$) with its 3-center-2-electron bonds.
- Carbon: Allotropes (Diamond, Graphite, Fullerenes) and silicates.

#### 2.2 Group 15 & 16
- Nitrogen: Ammonia ($NH_3$) and Nitric acid ($HNO_3$).
- Oxygen: Ozone ($O_3$) and Sulfuric acid ($H_2SO_4$).

#### 2.3 Group 17 & 18
- Halogens: Interhalogen compounds ($XX'_n$).
- Noble Gases: Xenon fluorides ($XeF_2, XeF_4, XeF_6$) and oxides.

## Formulas & Laws
- **Hydrolysis of Xenon Fluorides:** e.g., $6XeF_4 + 12H_2O \rightarrow 4Xe + 2XeO_3 + 24HF + 3O_2$.

## JEE Focus & Common Traps
- **Diborane Structure:** Understand the bridge bonds (banana bonds).
- **Xenon Compounds:** Shapes and hybridization (VSEPR) are frequently asked.
\n\n## Typed Problems (JEE Main PYQ Based)
### Type 1: Structures of Oxoacids
- **Q1.** Draw the structure of $H_3PO_4$ and find its basicity.
- **Concept:** Basicity is the number of replaceable $H$ atoms (attached to $O$).
- **Solution:**
  1. $H_3PO_4$ (Orthophosphoric acid) has three $P-OH$ bonds and one $P=O$ bond.
  2. Basicity = 3 (Tribasic).

### Type 2: Inert Pair Effect
- **Q1.** Why is $PbCl_2$ more stable than $PbCl_4$?
- **Concept:** As we move down the group, the $ns^2$ electrons become reluctant to participate in bonding due to poor shielding by $d$ and $f$ electrons.
- **Solution:**
  1. For heavier elements like $Pb$, the $+2$ oxidation state is more stable than $+4$.
  2. This is called the Inert Pair Effect.
