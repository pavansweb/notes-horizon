# Ionic Equilibrium

## Topic Breakdown
### 1. Acids, Bases and Salts
- Arrhenius, Bronsted-Lowry, and Lewis concepts.
- Dissociation constants ($K_a$ and $K_b$) and Degree of Dissociation ($\alpha$).

### 2. pH Scale and Hydrolysis of Salts
- Auto-ionization of water ($K_w = [H^+][OH^-]$).
- pH of different salt solutions: Strong Acid-Strong Base, Strong Acid-Weak Base, etc.

### 3. Buffer Solutions
- Acidic Buffers (Weak Acid + Salt with Strong Base).
- Basic Buffers (Weak Base + Salt with Strong Acid).

### 4. Solubility Product ($K_{sp}$)
- Relation between solubility ($S$) and $K_{sp}$.
- Condition for precipitation: Ionic Product ($IP$) $> K_{sp}$.

## Formulas & Laws
- **pH and pOH:** $pH = -\log[H^+]$, $pOH = -\log[OH^-]$, $pH + pOH = 14$ (at 298 K)
- **Ostwald's Dilution Law:** $\alpha = \sqrt{\frac{K}{C}}$ (for weak electrolytes)
- **Henderson-Hasselbalch Equation:** $pH = pK_a + \log \frac{[\text{Salt}]}{[\text{Acid}]}$ (for acidic buffer)
- **Solubility Product ($A_xB_y$):** $K_{sp} = x^x y^y S^{x+y}$

## JEE Focus & Common Traps
- **Common Ion Effect:** Solubility of a salt decreases in the presence of a common ion. Use this for selective precipitation problems.
- **Buffer Action:** Buffers are most effective when $pH = pK_a$ (i.e., $[\text{Salt}] = [\text{Acid}]$). The buffer range is typically $pK_a \pm 1$.
- **Approximate Calculations:** When $\alpha < 0.05$ (or $5\%$), $1 - \alpha \approx 1$. Always verify this after calculation.
\n\n## Typed Problems (JEE Main PYQ Based)
### Type 1: Buffer Solution pH
- **Q1.** Calculate the pH of a buffer solution containing $0.1 M$ $CH_3COOH$ and $0.1 M$ $CH_3COONa$. ($pK_a$ of acetic acid = $4.74$).
- **Concept:** Henderson-Hasselbalch equation: $pH = pK_a + \log\frac{[Salt]}{[Acid]}$.
- **Solution:**
  1. $pH = 4.74 + \log\frac{0.1}{0.1} = 4.74 + \log(1)$.
  2. $pH = 4.74 + 0 = 4.74$.

### Type 2: Solubility Product ($K_{sp}$)
- **Q1.** If the solubility of $AgCl$ in water is $10^{-5} mol/L$, calculate its $K_{sp}$.
- **Concept:** $K_{sp} = [Ag^+][Cl^-] = s^2$ for $AB$ type salt.
- **Solution:**
  1. $K_{sp} = (10^{-5})^2 = 10^{-10}$.
