# Hydrocarbons and Haloalkanes

## Topic Breakdown

### 1. Alkanes, Alkenes, and Alkynes
#### 1.1 Alkanes
- **Preparation**: Wurtz reaction, Kolbe's electrolysis, and decarboxylation.
- **Chemical Properties**: Free radical halogenation (mechanism and selectivity).
#### 1.2 Alkenes
- **Preparation**: Dehydration of alcohols and dehydrohalogenation of alkyl halides.
- **Addition Reactions**: Electrophilic addition ($HX, X_2, H_2O$), Hydroboration-oxidation, and Ozonolysis.
- **Stability**: Saytzeff vs. Hoffmann products.
#### 1.3 Alkynes
- **Preparation**: From gem-dihalides and vic-dihalides.
- **Chemical Properties**: Acidic nature of terminal alkynes; addition of $H_2$, $X_2$, and $H_2O$.

### 2. Aromatic Hydrocarbons (Arenes)
#### 2.1 Aromaticity
- **Huckel's Rule**: $4n+2$ $\pi$-electrons, planarity, and cyclic conjugation.
#### 2.2 Electrophilic Aromatic Substitution (EAS)
- **Mechanism**: Formation of $\sigma$-complex.
- **Reactions**: Nitration, Sulfonation, Halogenation, and Friedel-Crafts Alkylation/Acylation.
- **Directing Effects**: Ortho/Para vs. Meta directors.

### 3. Haloalkanes and Haloarenes
#### 3.1 Nucleophilic Substitution Reactions
- **$S_N2$ Mechanism**: Bimolecular, concerted, inversion of configuration (Walden Inversion). Favored by primary halides and polar aprotic solvents.
- **$S_N1$ Mechanism**: Unimolecular, two-step via carbocation, racemization. Favored by tertiary halides and polar protic solvents.
#### 3.2 Elimination Reactions
- **$E2$ and $E1$ Mechanisms**: Competition with substitution; effect of base strength and temperature.
#### 3.3 Organometallic Compounds
- **Grignard Reagents ($RMgX$)**: Preparation and versatility in synthesis.

## Formulas & Laws

### General Formulas
- **Alkanes**: $C_n H_{2n+2}$
- **Alkenes**: $C_n H_{2n}$
- **Alkynes**: $C_n H_{2n-2}$

### Reactivity Series
- **Leaving Group Ability**: $I^- > Br^- > Cl^- > F^-$
- **Carbocation Stability**: Tertiary > Secondary > Primary > Methyl

### Named Rules
- **Markovnikov's Rule**: In $HX$ addition to an unsymmetrical alkene, $X$ goes to the more substituted carbon.
- **Kharasch Effect**: Anti-Markovnikov addition of $HBr$ in the presence of peroxides.

## JEE Focus & Common Traps

- **SN1 vs SN2**: This is the most frequently tested area. Remember that $S_N2$ requires a clear path for back-side attack (steric hindrance is the enemy), while $S_N1$ depends entirely on the stability of the carbocation intermediate.
- **Peroxide Effect (HBr Only)**: The anti-Markovnikov addition via free radicals ONLY works with $HBr$. It does NOT work with $HCl$ or $HI$ due to thermodynamic reasons.
- **Carbocation Rearrangements**: In $S_N1$ and $E1$ reactions, always check if the carbocation can rearrange (1,2-hydride or 1,2-methyl shift) to a more stable form.
- **Aryl Halides Reactivity**: Aryl halides are extremely unreactive towards nucleophilic substitution due to resonance (partial double bond character of $C-X$) and instability of the phenyl cation.
- **Friedel-Crafts Limitations**: It does not work on strongly deactivated rings (like nitrobenzene) or with aniline (due to acid-base reaction with the catalyst).
- **Ozonolysis Interpretation**: Use ozonolysis products to reconstruct the original alkene. This is a common puzzle-style question in JEE.
- **Terminal Alkynes**: Remember their acidity. They react with $NaNH_2$ or Ammoniacal $AgNO_3$ (Tollens' reagent), which distinguishes them from non-terminal alkynes.
\n\n## Typed Problems (JEE Main PYQ Based)
### Type 1: Wurtz Reaction and Mechanisms
- **Q1.** Which alkane cannot be prepared in good yield by Wurtz reaction? (a) Butane (b) Hexane (c) 2,3-dimethylbutane (d) Heptane.
- **Concept:** Wurtz reaction is best for symmetrical alkanes with even number of carbons.
- **Solution:**
  1. Heptane is unsymmetrical (7 carbons).
  2. Using two different alkyl halides leads to a mixture of products ($C_6, C_7, C_8$), difficult to separate.
  3. Answer: (d).

### Type 2: Electrophilic Aromatic Substitution
- **Q1.** Predict the major product of nitration of Nitrobenzene.
- **Concept:** $-NO_2$ group is deactivating and meta-directing.
- **Solution:**
  1. The incoming electrophile $NO_2^+$ will attack the meta position.
  2. Product: m-dinitrobenzene.
