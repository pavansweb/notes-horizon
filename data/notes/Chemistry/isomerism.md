# Isomerism

## Topic Breakdown
### 1. Structural Isomerism
- Chain, Position, Functional, Metamerism, and Tautomerism (Keto-Enol).

### 2. Stereoisomerism
- **Geometrical Isomerism**: $cis/trans$ and $E/Z$ notation based on restricted rotation around double bonds or rings.
- **Optical Isomerism**: Chirality, Enantiomers, Diastereomers, and Meso compounds.

## Formulas & Laws
- **Double Bond Equivalent (DBE) / Degree of Unsaturation:** $DBE = C + 1 - \frac{H + X - N}{2}$
- **Optical Rotation:** $[\alpha]_{\lambda}^T = \frac{\alpha}{l \times c}$ (where $l$ is path length in dm and $c$ is concentration in g/mL)
- **Number of Stereoisomers:** $2^n$ (if molecule is unsymmetrical, where $n$ is number of chiral centers or double bonds showing GI)

## JEE Focus & Common Traps
- **Tautomerism:** Keto form is usually more stable than Enol form, except in cases like Phenol or where intramolecular H-bonding stabilizes the Enol.
- **Optical Activity:** A molecule with chiral centers can be optically inactive if it has a plane of symmetry or center of inversion (Meso compounds).
\n\n## Typed Problems (JEE Main PYQ Based)
### Type 1: Optical Isomerism
- **Q1.** Calculate the number of optical isomers for $CH_3CH(OH)CH(OH)CH_3$.
- **Concept:** For a molecule with $n$ chiral centers and symmetry, number of optical isomers is $2^{n-1}$.
- **Solution:**
  1. $n=2$. Chiral centers are $C2$ and $C3$.
  2. Molecule is symmetrical (Tartaric acid type).
  3. Isomers: $d, l$ (enantiomers) and $meso$. Total = 3.

### Type 2: Conformational Isomerism
- **Q1.** Which conformation of n-butane is most stable?
- **Concept:** Stability depends on torsional strain and steric hindrance (Van der Waals strain).
- **Solution:**
  1. Anti conformation: Methyl groups are $180^o$ apart, minimum repulsion.
  2. Order: Anti > Gauche > Partially Eclipsed > Fully Eclipsed.
