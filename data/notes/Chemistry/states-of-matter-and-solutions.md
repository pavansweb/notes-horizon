# States of Matter and Solutions

## Topic Breakdown
### 1. Gaseous State
#### 1.1 Ideal Gas Laws
- Boyle's Law ($P \propto 1/V$), Charles's Law ($V \propto T$), and Avogadro's Law ($V \propto n$).
- Ideal Gas Equation: $PV = nRT$.
#### 1.2 Kinetic Molecular Theory of Gases
- Postulates and derivation of $PV = \frac{1}{3} mNc^2$.
- Molecular Speeds: Root mean square ($u_{rms}$), Average ($u_{avg}$), and Most Probable ($u_{mp}$).
#### 1.3 Real Gases and Liquefaction
- Deviation from Ideal Behavior: Compressibility factor $Z = \frac{PV}{nRT}$.
- van der Waals Equation: $\left(P + \frac{an^2}{V^2}\right)(V - nb) = nRT$.
- Critical Constants: $T_c$, $P_c$, and $V_c$.

### 2. Liquid State
#### 2.1 Properties of Liquids
- Vapor Pressure, Surface Tension, and Viscosity.
- Temperature dependence of these properties.

### 3. Solutions and Colligative Properties
#### 3.1 Solubility and Concentration
- Henry's Law for gas-liquid solubility: $p = K_H x$.
- Raoult's Law for liquid-liquid solutions: $P_{total} = P_A^\circ x_A + P_B^\circ x_B$.
#### 3.2 Ideal and Non-Ideal Solutions
- Deviations from Raoult's Law (Positive and Negative).
- Azeotropes: Minimum and maximum boiling.
#### 3.3 Colligative Properties
- Relative Lowering of Vapor Pressure: $\frac{P_A^\circ - P_A}{P_A^\circ} = i x_B$.
- Elevation of Boiling Point: $\Delta T_b = i K_b m$.
- Depression of Freezing Point: $\Delta T_f = i K_f m$.
- Osmotic Pressure: $\Pi = iCRT$.

## Formulas & Laws
- **Ideal Gas Equation:** $PV = nRT$
- **van der Waals Equation:** $\left(P + \frac{an^2}{V^2}\right)(V - nb) = nRT$
- **Compressibility Factor:** $Z = \frac{PV}{nRT}$ ($Z=1$ for ideal, $Z>1$ for positive deviation, $Z<1$ for negative deviation)
- **Dalton's Law of Partial Pressures:** $P_{total} = \sum p_i$
- **Graham's Law of Effusion:** $\frac{r_1}{r_2} = \sqrt{\frac{M_2}{M_1}}$
- **Raoult's Law:** $p_i = p_i^\circ x_i$
- **van't Hoff Factor ($i$):** $i = \frac{\text{Observed Colligative Property}}{\text{Calculated Colligative Property}} = 1 + (n-1)\alpha$ (for dissociation)

## JEE Focus & Common Traps
- **Units:** Always use $T$ in Kelvin. In $PV=nRT$, match units of $P$ and $V$ with the correct value of $R$ ($0.0821\text{ L atm K}^{-1}\text{ mol}^{-1}$ or $8.314\text{ J K}^{-1}\text{ mol}^{-1}$).
- **van der Waals Constants:** 'a' measures intermolecular attractions; 'b' (co-volume) measures the effective size of molecules.
- **Ideal Solution Conditions:** $\Delta H_{mix} = 0$, $\Delta V_{mix} = 0$.
- **Colligative Property Calculations:** Don't forget the van't Hoff factor ($i$) for electrolytes. $i > 1$ for dissociation, $i < 1$ for association.
\n\n## Typed Problems (JEE Main PYQ Based)
### Type 1: Raoult's Law and Vapour Pressure
- **Q1.** The vapour pressure of pure liquids A and B are 450 and 700 mm Hg respectively. Find the composition of the liquid mixture if total vapour pressure is 600 mm Hg.
- **Concept:** $P_{total} = P^o_A X_A + P^o_B X_B$.
- **Solution:**
  1. $600 = 450 X_A + 700 (1 - X_A)$.
  2. $600 = 450 X_A + 700 - 700 X_A \implies 250 X_A = 100$.
  3. $X_A = 0.4, X_B = 0.6$.

### Type 2: Van't Hoff Factor ($i$)
- **Q1.** Calculate the Van't Hoff factor for $K_4[Fe(CN)_6]$ assuming $100\%$ dissociation.
- **Concept:** $i = 1 + \alpha(n-1)$.
- **Solution:**
  1. $K_4[Fe(CN)_6] \rightarrow 4K^+ + [Fe(CN)_6]^{4-}$.
  2. $n = 5, \alpha = 1$.
  3. $i = 1 + 1(5-1) = 5$.
