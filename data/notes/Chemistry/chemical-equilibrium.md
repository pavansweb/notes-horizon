# Chemical Equilibrium

## Topic Breakdown
### 1. Law of Mass Action and Equilibrium Constant
- Definition of $K_c$ and $K_p$.
- Relationship between $K_p$ and $K_c$: $K_p = K_c(RT)^{\Delta n_g}$.

### 2. Reaction Quotient ($Q$)
- Predicting reaction direction: $Q < K$ (forward), $Q > K$ (backward), $Q = K$ (equilibrium).

### 3. Le Chatelier's Principle
- Effects of concentration, pressure, temperature, and addition of inert gas on equilibrium position.

## Formulas & Laws
- **Equilibrium Constant Relation:** $K_p = K_c(RT)^{\Delta n_g}$
- **Gibbs Free Energy and Equilibrium:** $\Delta G^\circ = -RT \ln K = -2.303 RT \log K$

## JEE Focus & Common Traps
- **Temperature Dependence:** $K$ is constant unless temperature changes. Use Van't Hoff equation to find new $K$ at different $T$.
- **Solids and Pure Liquids:** The active mass (concentration) of pure solids and liquids is taken as 1 and does not appear in the $K$ expression.
\n\n## Typed Problems (JEE Main PYQ Based)
### Type 1: Relation between $K_p$ and $K_c$
- **Q1.** For the reaction $PCl_5(g) \rightleftharpoons PCl_3(g) + Cl_2(g)$, find the relation between $K_p$ and $K_c$.
- **Concept:** $K_p = K_c(RT)^{\Delta n_g}$, where $\Delta n_g$ is the change in number of moles of gaseous products and reactants.
- **Solution:**
  1. $\Delta n_g = (1 + 1) - 1 = 1$.
  2. Therefore, $K_p = K_c(RT)^1 = K_cRT$.

### Type 2: Le Chatelier's Principle
- **Q1.** What is the effect of increasing pressure on the equilibrium: $N_2(g) + 3H_2(g) \rightleftharpoons 2NH_3(g)$?
- **Concept:** Increasing pressure shifts equilibrium towards the side with fewer moles of gas.
- **Solution:**
  1. Reactant moles = $1+3=4$, Product moles = $2$.
  2. Increase in pressure shifts equilibrium to the right (forward), increasing $NH_3$ yield.
