# Amines, Biomolecules and Polymers

## Topic Breakdown
### 1. Amines
#### 1.1 Structure and Classification
- Primary ($1^\circ$), Secondary ($2^\circ$), and Tertiary ($3^\circ$) amines.
#### 1.2 Preparation and Basicity
- Gabriel Phthalimide Synthesis (for $1^\circ$ amines).
- Hoffmann Bromamide Degradation.
- Basicity: Aliphatic amines are more basic than $NH_3$ and aromatic amines. Order in aqueous solution: $2^\circ > 1^\circ > 3^\circ > NH_3$ (for methyl group).
#### 1.3 Chemical Reactions
- Carbylamine Reaction (Isocyanide test for $1^\circ$ amines).
- Hinsberg Test: Benzenesulfonyl chloride used to distinguish $1^\circ, 2^\circ, 3^\circ$ amines.
- Diazotization: Formation of Benzenediazonium chloride ($0-5^\circ C$).

### 2. Biomolecules
#### 2.1 Carbohydrates
- Classification: Monosaccharides (Glucose, Fructose), Disaccharides (Sucrose, Maltose, Lactose), Polysaccharides (Starch, Cellulose).
- Reducing vs. Non-reducing Sugars.
#### 2.2 Proteins and Amino Acids
- $\alpha$-Amino acids: Essential and Non-essential.
- Zwitterion structure and Isoelectric point ($pI$).
- Peptide Bond: $-CO-NH-$ linkage.
- Protein Structure: Primary, Secondary ($\alpha$-helix, $\beta$-pleated sheet), Tertiary, and Quaternary.
#### 2.3 Nucleic Acids
- DNA and RNA: Nucleotides, Nucleosides, and Hydrogen bonding ($A=T, G \equiv C$).

### 3. Polymers
#### 3.1 Classification and Types
- Addition (Chain-growth) vs. Condensation (Step-growth) polymerization.
- Natural (Rubber, Cellulose) and Synthetic (Nylon, Polyester) polymers.
#### 3.2 Important Polymers
- Fibers: Nylon-6,6, Terylene (Dacron).
- Plastics: Polythene, Bakelite, Melamine.
- Elastomers: Buna-S, Buna-N, Neoprene.

## Formulas & Laws
- **Basicity Constant ($K_b$):** $pK_b = -\log K_b$ (Smaller $pK_b$ means stronger base).
- **Isoelectric Point ($pI$):** $pI = \frac{pK_{a1} + pK_{a2}}{2}$ (for simple amino acids).
- **Glucose Structure:** Open chain vs. Cyclic (Haworth structures).
- **Specific Rotation of Sugars:** Calculation of concentration or purity using polarimetry.

## JEE Focus & Common Traps
- **Amine Basicity Order:** Be careful with the solvent (gas phase vs. aqueous) and the alkyl group (Methyl vs. Ethyl).
- **Hinsberg Reagent:** $1^\circ$ amines give a product soluble in alkali; $2^\circ$ give a product insoluble in alkali; $3^\circ$ do not react.
- **Sucrose:** It is a non-reducing sugar because both functional groups are involved in the glycosidic linkage.
- **Buna-S and Buna-N:** Buna-S is a copolymer of 1,3-butadiene and styrene; Buna-N is a copolymer of 1,3-butadiene and acrylonitrile.
\n\n## Typed Problems (JEE Main PYQ Based)
### Type 1: Basicity of Amines
- **Q1.** Arrange the following amines in increasing order of basic strength in aqueous solution: $(CH_3)_2NH, CH_3NH_2, (CH_3)_3N, NH_3$.
- **Concept:** Basicity in aqueous solution depends on Inductive effect, Solvation effect, and Steric hindrance.
- **Solution:**
  1. For methyl substituted amines in water, the order is determined by a balance of factors.
  2. Order: $NH_3 < (CH_3)_3N < CH_3NH_2 < (CH_3)_2NH$.
  3. Experimental $pK_b$ values support $(CH_3)_2NH$ as the strongest base due to optimal solvation and inductive effect.

### Type 2: Carbohydrates and Reducing Sugars
- **Q1.** Which of the following is a non-reducing sugar? (a) Glucose (b) Maltose (c) Lactose (d) Sucrose.
- **Concept:** Reducing sugars have a free aldehydic or ketonic group (hemiacetal/hemiketal form). In Sucrose, the glycosidic linkage involves the anomeric carbons of both glucose and fructose.
- **Solution:**
  1. Sucrose is formed by $\alpha$-D-glucose and $\beta$-D-fructose.
  2. Linkage is $C1$ of glucose and $C2$ of fructose.
  3. Both reducing groups are involved in the bond, hence it's non-reducing.
