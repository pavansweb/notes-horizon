# Practical Chemistry and Qualitative Analysis

## Topic Breakdown
### 1. Organic Qualitative Analysis
#### 1.1 Detection of Extra Elements (Lassaigne's Test)
- Nitrogen, Sulfur, and Halogens are detected by fusing the organic compound with Sodium metal.
- **Nitrogen:** Prussian blue color with $FeSO_4$ and $FeCl_3$.
- **Sulfur:** Violet color with Sodium Nitroprusside.

#### 1.2 Identification of Functional Groups
- **Hydroxyl (-OH):** Sodium metal test (Hydrogen gas evolution).
- **Carbonyl (>C=O):** 2,4-DNP test (Orange/Yellow precipitate).
- **Carboxyl (-COOH):** Sodium bicarbonate test ($CO_2$ effervescence).
- **Amino (-NH2):** Carbylamine test (Foul smell of Isocyanide).

### 2. Inorganic Qualitative Analysis (Salt Analysis)
#### 2.1 Detection of Anions (Acid Radicals)
- **Group I (Dilute $H_2SO_4$):** $CO_3^{2-}, S^{2-}, SO_3^{2-}, NO_2^-$.
- **Group II (Conc. $H_2SO_4$):** $Cl^-, Br^-, I^-, NO_3^-, CH_3COO^-$.

#### 2.2 Detection of Cations (Basic Radicals)
- **Group 0:** $NH_4^+$ (Nessler's Reagent test).
- **Group I:** $Pb^{2+}$ (Chloride precipitate).
- **Group II:** $Cu^{2+}, Cd^{2+}$ (Sulfide precipitate in acidic medium).
- **Group III:** $Fe^{3+}, Al^{3+}$ (Hydroxide precipitate using $NH_4Cl$ and $NH_4OH$).

### 3. Volumetric Analysis
#### 3.1 Titrations
- **Redox Titrations:** $KMnO_4$ vs Oxalic acid; $K_2Cr_2O_7$ vs Mohr's salt.
- **Acid-Base Titrations:** Indicators like Phenolphthalein and Methyl Orange.

## Formulas & Laws
- **Molarity ($M$):** $M = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{Volume of solution (L)}}$
- **Equivalent Weight:** $E = \frac{\text{Molar Mass}}{n\text{-factor}}$

## JEE Focus & Common Traps
- **Interfering Radicals:** Fluoride, Borate, and Phosphate can interfere with cation analysis and must be removed.
- **Flame Test:** $Li$ (Crimson), $Na$ (Yellow), $K$ (Violet), $Ca$ (Brick Red).
\n\n## Typed Problems (JEE Main PYQ Based)
### Type 1: Lassaigne's Test
- **Q1.** In Lassaigne's test for nitrogen, the Prussian blue color is due to the formation of?
- **Concept:** Reaction of sodium fusion extract with $FeSO_4$ and $FeCl_3$.
- **Solution:**
  1. $6CN^- + Fe^{2+} \rightarrow [Fe(CN)_6]^{4-}$.
  2. $3[Fe(CN)_6]^{4-} + 4Fe^{3+} \rightarrow Fe_4[Fe(CN)_6]_3$.
  3. Compound is Ferri-ferrocyanide ($Fe_4[Fe(CN)_6]_3$).

### Type 2: Cation Analysis (Group III)
- **Q1.** Why is $NH_4Cl$ added before $NH_4OH$ in Group III analysis?
- **Concept:** Common ion effect to suppress hydroxide concentration.
- **Solution:**
  1. Group III radicals ($Al^{3+}, Fe^{3+}, Cr^{3+}$) have very low $K_{sp}$ for hydroxides.
  2. $NH_4Cl$ provides $NH_4^+$ which shifts $NH_4OH \rightleftharpoons NH_4^+ + OH^-$ to the left.
  3. This prevents precipitation of Group IV and V cations as hydroxides.
