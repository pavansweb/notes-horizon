# General Organic Chemistry

## Topic Breakdown
### 1. Electronic Effects
#### 1.1 Inductive Effect ($+I$ and $-I$)
- Permanent displacement of $\sigma$ electrons along a saturated carbon chain.
- $-I$ Groups: $-NO_2, -CN, -COOH, -F, -Cl, -Br, -I$.
- $+I$ Groups: $-CH_3, -CH_2CH_3, -(CH_3)_2CH, -(CH_3)_3C$.
#### 1.2 Resonance (Mesomeric) Effect ($+M$ and $-M$)
- Polarity produced by the interaction between two $\pi$-bonds or a $\pi$-bond and a lone pair.
- Conditions for Resonance: Conjugation, planarity.
#### 1.3 Hyperconjugation
- Delocalization of $\sigma$ electrons of $C-H$ bond of an alkyl group directly attached to an atom of unshared $\pi$ orbital.

### 2. Reaction Intermediates
#### 2.1 Carbocations
- $sp^2$ hybridized, trigonal planar. Stability: Tertiary $>$ Secondary $>$ Primary.
#### 2.2 Carbanions
- $sp^3$ hybridized (usually), pyramidal. Stability: Primary $>$ Secondary $>$ Tertiary.
#### 2.3 Free Radicals
- Paramagnetic species, $sp^2$ hybridized.

### 3. Aromaticity
#### 3.1 Huckel's Rule
- Aromatic: Cyclic, planar, complete conjugation, $(4n+2)\pi$ electrons.
- Anti-aromatic: Cyclic, planar, complete conjugation, $4n\pi$ electrons.

## Formulas & Laws
- **Huckel's Rule:** Aromatic if $\pi \text{ electrons} = 4n + 2$ (where $n = 0, 1, 2, ...$)

## JEE Focus & Common Traps
- **Stability Ranking:** Always check Resonance $>$ Hyperconjugation $>$ Inductive effect ($R > H > I$).
- **Resonance Structures:** The most contributing structure is the one with the maximum number of covalent bonds and minimum formal charge separation.
\n\n## Typed Problems (JEE Main PYQ Based)
### Type 1: Carbocation Stability
- **Q1.** Arrange in decreasing order of stability: (i) $(CH_3)_3C^+$, (ii) $(CH_3)_2CH^+$, (iii) $CH_3CH_2^+$, (iv) $CH_3^+$.
- **Concept:** Stability is increased by $+I$ effect and Hyperconjugation (number of $\alpha$-hydrogens).
- **Solution:**
  1. (i) has 9 $\alpha$-H, (ii) has 6 $\alpha$-H, (iii) has 3 $\alpha$-H, (iv) has 0.
  2. Order: (i) > (ii) > (iii) > (iv).

### Type 2: Tautomerism
- **Q1.** Which of the following will show tautomerism? (a) Benzophenone, (b) Acetaldehyde, (c) Benzaldehyde.
- **Concept:** Presence of $\alpha$-hydrogen adjacent to a carbonyl group is required.
- **Solution:**
  1. Acetaldehyde ($CH_3CHO$) has 3 $\alpha$-hydrogens.
  2. Benzophenone and Benzaldehyde have no $\alpha$-hydrogens.
  3. Answer: (b).
