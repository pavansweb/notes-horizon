# Periodic Table and Chemical Bonding

## Topic Breakdown

### 1. Classification of Elements and Periodicity
#### 1.1 Modern Periodic Law
- Elements are arranged by increasing atomic number.
- Division into s, p, d, and f blocks.
#### 1.2 Periodic Trends
- **Atomic and Ionic Radii**: Generally decreases across a period, increases down a group.
- **Ionization Enthalpy ($IE$)**: Energy required to remove an electron.
- **Electron Gain Enthalpy ($\Delta_{eg}H$)**: Energy change when an electron is added.
- **Electronegativity**: Ability to attract shared electrons (Pauling scale).

### 2. Chemical Bonding Fundamentals
#### 2.1 Ionic Bonding
- Formation via electron transfer; Lattice Energy and Born-Haber Cycle.
- **Fajan's Rules**: Predicting covalent character in ionic bonds.
#### 2.2 Covalent Bonding
- Lewis dot structures and Octet rule (including exceptions).
- **Formal Charge**: Assessing the stability of Lewis structures.

### 3. VSEPR Theory and Hybridization
#### 3.1 Valence Shell Electron Pair Repulsion (VSEPR)
- Predicting molecular shapes based on electron pair repulsions (lp-lp > lp-bp > bp-bp).
#### 3.2 Valence Bond Theory (VBT) and Hybridization
- Orbital overlapping and the formation of $sp, sp^2, sp^3, sp^3d, sp^3d^2$ hybrid orbitals.
- Bond parameters: Bond length, bond angle, and bond enthalpy.

### 4. Molecular Orbital Theory (MOT)
#### 4.1 LCAO Method
- Formation of Bonding and Antibonding molecular orbitals.
#### 4.2 Electronic Configuration of Molecules
- Predicting Bond Order and magnetic behavior (diamagnetic vs. paramagnetic).

### 5. Intermolecular Forces
- **Hydrogen Bonding**: Types (Inter- and Intra-molecular) and effects on physical properties.
- **Van der Waals Forces**: Dispersion, Dipole-Dipole, and Ion-Dipole interactions.

## JEE Focus & Common Traps

- **Ionization Enthalpy Exceptions**: Remember the stability of half-filled and fully-filled subshells. Nitrogen ($1s^2 2s^2 2p^3$) has a higher $IE_1$ than Oxygen ($1s^2 2s^2 2p^4$) due to its stable half-filled p-orbital.
- **Electron Gain Enthalpy of Halogens**: Chlorine has a more negative $\Delta_{eg}H$ than Fluorine due to Fluorine's small size and high inter-electronic repulsions in the $2p$ subshell.
- **VSEPR Shapes**: Differentiate between "Electron Geometry" (including lone pairs) and "Molecular Shape" (only atoms). For example, $NH_3$ has tetrahedral electron geometry but pyramidal molecular shape.
- **MOT and Magnetism**: Oxygen ($O_2$) is paramagnetic due to two unpaired electrons in $\pi^*$ orbitals. This is a classic question that Lewis structures fail to explain.
- **Fajan's Rules**: Small cation, large anion, and high charge favor covalency. For example, $LiCl$ is more covalent than $NaCl$.
- **Bond Angles**: Compare angles systematically: Hybridization $	o$ Number of lone pairs $	o$ Electronegativity of central atom $	o$ Size of surrounding atoms.
- **Back Bonding**: Understand the impact of back-bonding (e.g., in $BF_3$) on bond length and acidity.
\n\n## Typed Problems (JEE Main PYQ Based)
### Type 1: VSEPR and Hybridization
- **Q1.** Predict the shape and hybridization of $XeF_4$.
- **Concept:** $Steric Number = \frac{1}{2}[V + M - C + A]$.
- **Solution:**
  1. For $XeF_4: S.N. = \frac{1}{2}[8 + 4] = 6$.
  2. Hybridization: $sp^3d^2$.
  3. 4 bond pairs, 2 lone pairs $\implies$ Square Planar shape.

### Type 2: Fajan's Rule
- **Q1.** Which has more covalent character: $LiCl$ or $NaCl$?
- **Concept:** Smaller cation and larger anion favor covalent character.
- **Solution:**
  1. $Li^+$ is smaller than $Na^+$.
  2. $Li^+$ has higher polarizing power.
  3. Therefore, $LiCl$ is more covalent.
