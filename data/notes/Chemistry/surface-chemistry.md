# Surface Chemistry

## Topic Breakdown
### 1. Adsorption
- **Physisorption vs. Chemisorption**: Bonding nature and heat of adsorption.
- **Adsorption Isotherms**: Freundlich and Langmuir models.

### 2. Catalysis
- **Homogeneous and Heterogeneous Catalysis**.
- **Enzyme Catalysis**: Lock-and-key mechanism.

### 3. Colloids
- **Classification**: Based on physical state, dispersion medium, and interaction (Lyophilic/Lyophobic).
- **Properties**: Tyndall effect, Brownian motion, and Electrophoresis.
- **Stability**: Zeta potential and coagulation (Hardy-Schulze Rule).

## Formulas & Laws
- **Freundlich Isotherm**: $\frac{x}{m} = k P^{1/n}$
- **Hardy-Schulze Rule**: Coagulating power $\propto (\text{valency of ion})^4$ (approx).

## JEE Focus & Common Traps
- **Physisorption vs. Chemisorption**: Chemisorption is highly specific and increases with temperature initially (due to activation energy), whereas physisorption always decreases with temperature.
- **Gold Number**: Smaller the gold number, greater is the protective power of the lyophilic colloid.
- **Hardy-Schulze Rule**: When coagulating a negative sol (like $As_2S_3$), the order of power of cations is $Al^{3+} > Ba^{2+} > Na^+$.
\n\n## Typed Problems (JEE Main PYQ Based)
### Type 1: Adsorption Isotherms
- **Q1.** In Freundlich adsorption isotherm, what is the slope of the plot $\log(x/m)$ vs $\log P$?
- **Concept:** $\frac{x}{m} = k P^{1/n} \implies \log(x/m) = \log k + \frac{1}{n} \log P$.
- **Solution:**
  1. The equation is in $y = mx + c$ form.
  2. Slope = $1/n$.

### Type 2: Hardy-Schulze Rule
- **Q1.** Which ion has the highest coagulating power for a negatively charged $As_2S_3$ sol? (a) $Na^+$ (b) $Mg^{2+}$ (c) $Al^{3+}$.
- **Concept:** Higher the valency of the flocculating ion, the greater is its power to cause precipitation.
- **Solution:**
  1. For a negative sol, we need a cation.
  2. Valency order: $Al^{3+} > Mg^{2+} > Na^+$.
  3. Answer: (c).
