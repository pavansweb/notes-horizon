# Alcohols, Phenols, Ethers and Carbonyl Compounds

## Topic Breakdown

### 1. Alcohols and Phenols
#### 1.1 Classification and Nomenclature
- Monohydric, dihydric, and polyhydric alcohols.
- IUPAC and common naming systems.
#### 1.2 Preparation Methods
- **Hydration of Alkenes**: Acid-catalyzed, Oxymercuration-demercuration, and Hydroboration-oxidation.
- **Reduction**: Carbonyl compounds using $LiAlH_4$, $NaBH_4$, or catalytic hydrogenation.
- **Grignard Reagents**: Addition to aldehydes and ketones.
#### 1.3 Physical Properties
- Hydrogen bonding, boiling point trends, and solubility.
#### 1.4 Chemical Properties
- **Acidity**: Comparison between alcohols and phenols; effect of substituents.
- **Esterification**: Reaction with carboxylic acids and derivatives.
- **Dehydration**: Mechanism and Saytzeff vs. Hoffmann products.
- **Oxidation**: Primary $	o$ Aldehyde/Acid, Secondary $	o$ Ketone.

### 2. Ethers
#### 2.1 Preparation
- **Williamson Ether Synthesis**: $R-X + R'-O^-Na^+ 	o R-O-R'$.
- **Dehydration of Alcohols**.
#### 2.2 Reactivity
- Cleavage by $HX$ (mechanism depends on $R$ groups).
- Ring opening of epoxides.

### 3. Carbonyl Compounds (Aldehydes and Ketones)
#### 3.1 Preparation
- Oxidation of alcohols, ozonolysis of alkenes, and hydration of alkynes.
- **Rosenmund Reduction** and **Stephen's Reaction**.
#### 3.2 Nucleophilic Addition Reactions
- Addition of $HCN$, $NaHSO_3$, Grignard reagents, and alcohols (acetal/ketal formation).
- Addition of ammonia derivatives (oximes, hydrazones).
#### 3.3 Oxidation and Reduction
- **Tollens' and Fehling's Tests**: Identifying aldehydes.
- **Clemensen and Wolff-Kishner Reductions**.
#### 3.4 Named Reactions
- **Aldol Condensation**: Requiring $\alpha$-hydrogen.
- **Cannizzaro Reaction**: Aldehydes without $\alpha$-hydrogen.
- **Haloform Reaction**: Test for methyl ketones.

## Formulas & Laws

### Acidity and Basicity
- **Phenol Acidity**: Increased by electron-withdrawing groups ($-NO_2, -CN, -X$) and decreased by electron-donating groups ($-R, -OR$).
- **Lucas Test**: $ROH + HCl/ZnCl_2$. Primary (no turbidity), Secondary (5 mins), Tertiary (immediate).

### Mechanism Rules
- **Markovnikov's Rule**: In addition to alkenes, $H$ goes to the carbon with more $H$'s.
- **Saytzeff's Rule**: Elimination favors the more substituted alkene.

### Stoichiometry in Reactions
- **Iodoform Test**: $R-CO-CH_3 + 3I_2 + 4NaOH 	o R-COONa + CHI_3 + 3NaI + 3H_2O$

## JEE Focus & Common Traps

- **Grignard Reagent Limitations**: It cannot be used in the presence of acidic hydrogens ($-OH, -NH_2, -COOH, -C \equiv C-H$).
- **Cleavage of Ethers with HI**: If one group is tertiary, the mechanism is $S_N1$ (iodide goes to tertiary C). If both are primary/secondary, it's $S_N2$ (iodide goes to smaller group).
- **Aldol vs. Cannizzaro**: Check for $\alpha$-hydrogens carefully. Even if one reactant has $\alpha$-H and the other doesn't, it's a Cross-Aldol.
- **Oxidation with PCC vs. $KMnO_4$**: PCC stops at the aldehyde stage for primary alcohols, while $KMnO_4/K_2Cr_2O_7$ oxidizes it further to carboxylic acid.
- **Phenol vs. Aliphatic Alcohol**: Phenol is much more acidic than aliphatic alcohols due to resonance stabilization of the phenoxide ion. It reacts with $NaOH$ while ethanol does not.
- **Formaldehyde in Cannizzaro**: In a mixed Cannizzaro with another aldehyde, formaldehyde is always oxidized to formic acid because of its higher reactivity.

### Type 1: Aldol Condensation and Cannizzaro Reaction
- **Q1.** An organic compound (A) with molecular formula $C_8H_8O$ forms an orange-red precipitate with 2,4-DNP reagent and gives a yellow precipitate on heating with iodine in the presence of sodium hydroxide. It neither reduces Tollen's nor Fehling's reagent. On drastic oxidation with chromic acid, it gives a carboxylic acid (B) having molecular formula $C_7H_6O_2$. Identify (A) and (B).
- **Concept:** Carbonyl compounds with $\alpha$-hydrogen undergo Aldol condensation, while those without $\alpha$-hydrogen (and being aldehydes) undergo Cannizzaro. Methyl ketones give the Iodoform test.
- **Solution:**
  1. (A) has formula $C_8H_8O$. It forms 2,4-DNP derivative $\implies$ contains a carbonyl group.
  2. Gives yellow ppt with $I_2/NaOH \implies$ methyl ketone group ($-COCH_3$).
  3. Does not reduce Tollen's/Fehling's $\implies$ not an aldehyde.
  4. Oxidation gives $C_7H_6O_2$ (Benzoic acid) $\implies$ (A) is Acetophenone ($C_6H_5COCH_3$).
  5. Reaction: $C_6H_5COCH_3 + 3I_2 + 4NaOH \rightarrow C_6H_5COONa + CHI_3 \downarrow + 3NaI + 3H_2O$.

### Type 2: Acidic Strength of Phenols
- **Q1.** Arrange the following in increasing order of acidic strength: o-nitrophenol, m-nitrophenol, p-nitrophenol, and phenol.
- **Concept:** Electron-withdrawing groups (EWG) like $-NO_2$ increase acidity by stabilizing the phenoxide ion via $-I$ and $-M$ effects.
- **Solution:**
  1. Nitro group is EWG. Its effect is maximum at ortho and para due to $-M$ and $-I$.
  2. At meta, only $-I$ effect operates.
  3. In o-nitrophenol, intramolecular hydrogen bonding slightly decreases acidity compared to p-nitrophenol.
  4. Order: Phenol < m-nitrophenol < o-nitrophenol < p-nitrophenol.
