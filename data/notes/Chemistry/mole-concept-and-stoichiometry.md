## Formulas & Laws
- **Moles:** $n = \frac{\text{Given Mass (m)}}{\text{Molar Mass (M)}} = \frac{\text{No. of Particles (N)}}{N_A} = \frac{\text{Volume at STP (L)}}{22.4}$
- **Molarity (M):** $M = \frac{\text{Moles of Solute}}{\text{Volume of Solution (L)}}$
- **Molality (m):** $m = \frac{\text{Moles of Solute}}{\text{Mass of Solvent (kg)}}$
- **Mole Fraction (\chi):** \chi_A = \frac{n_A}{n_A + n_B}$
- **Normality (N):** $N = M \times \text{n-factor} = \frac{\text{Equivalents of Solute}}{\text{Volume of Solution (L)}}$
- **Dilution Law:** $M_1V_1 = M_2V_2$
- **Ideal Gas Law:** $PV = nRT$

## JEE Focus & Common Traps
- **Limiting Reagent:** Never assume the reactant with the least mass is the limiting reagent. Always compare the ratio of moles to stoichiometric coefficients.
- **Temperature Dependence:** Molarity, Normality, and Volume Percent depend on temperature because they involve volume. Molality, Mole Fraction, and Mass Percent are temperature-independent.
- **n-factor in Redox:** Be careful with the n-factor of $\text{KMnO}_4$ and $\text{K}_2\text{Cr}_2\text{O}_7$. For $\text{KMnO}_4$: $n=5$ (acidic), $n=1$ (strongly basic), $n=3$ (neutral/weakly basic).
- **Equivalent Weight:** $E = \frac{M}{\text{n-factor}}$. Remember that equivalents always react in a 1:1 ratio, unlike moles.
- **Density in Concentration:** When converting from molarity to molality, remember that the density of the solution relates mass and volume: $\rho = \frac{\text{Mass}}{\text{Volume}}$.

### Type 1: Limiting Reagent
- **Q1.** $50 kg$ of $N_2(g)$ and $10 kg$ of $H_2(g)$ are mixed to produce $NH_3(g)$. Identify the limiting reagent.
- **Concept:** The reactant that is consumed completely and limits the amount of product.
- **Solution:**
  1. $N_2 + 3H_2 \rightarrow 2NH_3$.
  2. Moles of $N_2 = 50000/28 \approx 1785.7$.
  3. Moles of $H_2 = 10000/2 = 5000$.
  4. Required $H_2$ for $1785.7$ moles $N_2 = 1785.7 \times 3 = 5357.1$.
  5. We have only $5000$ moles $H_2$, so $H_2$ is the limiting reagent.

### Type 2: Concentration Terms
- **Q1.** Calculate the molality of $2.5 g$ of ethanoic acid ($CH_3COOH$) in $75 g$ of benzene.
- **Concept:** $Molality (m) = \frac{moles of solute}{mass of solvent in kg}$.
- **Solution:**
  1. Moles of $CH_3COOH = 2.5 / 60 \approx 0.0417$.
  2. Mass of benzene = $0.075 kg$.
  3. $m = 0.0417 / 0.075 = 0.556 mol/kg$.
